Tower Bridge in London, England
The Tower Bridge is one of the main symbols of London and Great Britain, harmoniously blending into the architecture of the city center and attracting hundreds of tourists every day. Located Tower Bridge, in the historic center of the city near the Tower of London, on the north bank of the River Thames, hence its name.

Brighton beach...
Brighton is one of the most famous resorts in the world and, undoubtedly, the sea resort of England No. 1, is located on the southern coast of the country, in the county of East Sussex. Despite the fact that the historical records mark the existence of the settlement in this place already in 1086, the rapid development of Brighton as a sea resort dates back to the mid to late 18th century, when the London doctor Richard Russell began recommending this point on the coast of England for the adoption of "marine Baths ", very useful for health.

Briefly about the Adriatic Coast
The Adriatic coast in Italy is rightly considered one of the best places for a beach holiday. This is not only about Rimini, well-known to Russians: along the coast scattered a lot of original resort towns with luxurious beaches and excellent infrastructure: Milano Marittima, Cattolica, Gabicce, Pesaro, Cesenatico, etc.

City of canals and bridges
The history of the city of Venice is more than 1,500 years old. It all began with a small village, founded by the inhabitants of the region on deserted marshy islands, which escaped from the invasion of the Goths. According to archaeological excavations, this event occurred in 421 AD. Gradually the village grew and soon turned into a real city.

Gran Vía street
The first project of this street, which was to connect the street of Alcala from the Plaza de España, dated 1862 year. At one time the project of building a new street was one of the main themes of the life of Madrid. Its construction included the disappearance of 14 streets, the reconstruction of 34 streets and the demolition of three hundred old houses.

Northern Railway Station
The northern train station in Valencia, from which trains leave almost to all the major cities of Spain, is considered an architectural gem in the Art Nouveau style. The station building was built in 1906-1917 in the city center. Its facade is decorated with three towers, ceramics and mosaics, more reminiscent of paintings, and also decorated with wood, glass and metal.

More than the Eiffel Tower
There are men's countries, and there are definitely women's. This is France. Contradictory, mysterious, attractive. Probably, this is the most romantic country in the world. Attractive perfume, fashionable clothes, chanson sounds, exquisite cuisine, expensive wine and sensual French, intended, it seems, only for explanations of love. France is Cannes with a deafening nightlife and a luxurious public, but it's quiet neat little towns where they speak German dialect. And the Kamarga marshes next to the sand dunes over which fly the flamingo, the dazzling Nice, the wild and misty plains of Alsace.

Pont Alexandre III (Paris)
The Alexander III Bridge in Paris extends between the House of the Disabled and the Champs-Elysées. In order not to obscure the panorama of the Champs Elysees, the bridge was made not more than 6 meters high, which at the time of its construction was considered an amazing achievement. The bridge was laid in commemoration of the Franco-Russian Union by Emperor Nicholas II in October 1896 and erected in four years. The name of the bridge was given in honor of his father - Emperor Alexander III.

Bridge Manger Brug
The bridge was first built in this place in 1691. He was so small that he deserved the name "skinny" or "bridge-thin." Subsequently, it was repeatedly rebuilt and rebuilt. In 1969, the last restoration work was carried out. They did not change the appearance of the bridge - it looks almost the same as 300 years ago, and painted in the same white color.

City Museum of Amsterdam (Stadelek-Museum)
At the very end of the XIX century in Amsterdam, there was a significant event for the city. At the wasteland in the southern part of the city were built just three buildings, which now form the Museum Square and play a crucial role in the life not only of Amsterdam, but of the whole kingdom. It is the State Museum (Rijksmuseum, 1885), the Concert Hall (1888) and the City Museum (Stedelek-Museum, 1895).
